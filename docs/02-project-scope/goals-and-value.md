# Goals & Value

Problem statement and value proposition for the Charging Station Locator.

## The Problem

**Who needs this?**

Millions of vulnerable people lack access to reliable device charging:

- **People experiencing homelessness** — Phones are critical for safety, employment, and accessing shelter/benefits
- **Low-income travelers** — Limited resources for paid charging stations
- **Students** — Between-class charging gaps on campus
- **Gig workers** — Need phones charged for work availability
- **Other vulnerable populations** — Particularly those without private/home charging access

**The challenge:**

Information about free charging is scattered across multiple websites, often hidden behind paywalls, or sold to data brokers. No simple, privacy-first solution exists.

**The impact:**

A dead phone means:
- Lost contact with shelters
- Missed job opportunities
- Inability to reach emergency services
- Social isolation

## Our Solution

**Charging Station Locator:** A simple, open, privacy-respecting map showing where free charging is available, right now.

### Key Principles

1. **Privacy-First** — No accounts, no tracking, no data collection
2. **Accessible** — Works on any device with a browser
3. **Simple** — One clear feature: find nearby charging
4. **Sustainable** — Free to host, free to use
5. **Community-Driven** — Open-source foundation for local communities

## Value Created

### 1. **Immediate Utility**
Users can see charging stations near them in real-time. No signup, no login, no friction.

### 2. **Privacy Protection**
Unlike commercial alternatives:
- No user accounts (no data theft risk)
- No tracking (no surveillance capitalism)
- No data collection (no third-party sales)
- Fully client-side (no server-side data storage)

### 3. **Accessibility**
Works on:
- Desktop computers (full experience)
- Tablets (responsive layout)
- Low-end smartphones (lightweight, fast)
- Any device with a web browser

### 4. **Community Foundation**
Open-source base that communities can fork and extend:
- Add their own local stations
- Integrate with existing resources
- No vendor lock-in
- Full transparency

### 5. **Zero Barrier to Entry**
- Just visit the URL (https://plug.vln.gg)
- No installation required
- No signup process
- No download needed
- Instant access

### 6. **Sustainable Model**
- Zero infrastructure costs (GitHub Pages is free)
- No backend to maintain
- No servers to scale
- No subscriptions to manage
- Can scale to millions of users

## Impact Metrics

**For vulnerable populations:**
- ✅ Helps maintain connectivity (critical for safety/employment)
- ✅ Supports independence
- ✅ Reduces digital divide
- ✅ Democratizes access to information
- ✅ Demonstrates technology can serve those left behind

**For communities:**
- ✅ Replicable model (can add any city)
- ✅ No dependency on corporate platforms
- ✅ Community-owned resource
- ✅ Extensible foundation (add submissions, verification, etc.)

**For technology:**
- ✅ Proves simple solutions work
- ✅ Demonstrates GitHub Pages capabilities (full SPA without backend)
- ✅ Shows privacy-first design is feasible
- ✅ Provides reusable pattern for community mapping

## Why This Matters

**Beyond features:**

This isn't a to-do list app or weather widget. It addresses a real need for real people. A person experiencing homelessness can visit plug.vln.gg and immediately find where they can charge their phone—potentially saving them from lost shelter placement, missed job call, or being unable to call for help.

**Bootcamp-grade engineering solving real-world problems:**

We prove that seven-day development sprints can create meaningful impact, not just practice projects.
